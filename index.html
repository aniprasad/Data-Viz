Manchester United Football Club, is a professional football club based in Old Trafford, Manchester, United Kingdom. It was founded as Newton Heath LYR in 1878. Manchester United stands as a beacon of sporting brilliance, boasting a record of triumphs and trophies that have left an inedible mark on the game. Take a look at the chart above to see just a few of their achievements. They are the most successful English football team, having won 20 Premier league titles more than any other team. They were also the first English club to win the historic treble, winning the Premier League, the F.A cup, and the coveted UEFA Champions League in 1999. Readers can also hover over some of the circles, to see extra information so they can familiarize themselves with what Manchester United is all about.

Dive into this visualization by looking at Manchester United's performance over a period of 11 years from 2006 to 2017. This visualization covers home performance, as well as season performance, allowing viewers to notice how much of a role home performance plays in a team doing well. Viewers can also notice the dips in performance in certain seasons due to managerial changes as denoted by the annotation lines. Further more, the viewer can also toggle between seasons via the dropdown to look at statistics pertaining to that season. Click "Next" to begin the journey!

This chart showcases Manchester United's home performance over a period of 10 years. It details each team they played against, the number of home goals scored and conceded (which is color coded for the convenience of the reader) and the result of the match (which is placed on top of the stacked bar chart). On hover, it also show cases, the individual stats of each match, namely the goals scored, conceded, and the result. Readers can quickly toggle between various seasons using the dropdown

Readers are encourage to toggle between seasons, to look at helpful annotations. For example, in the 2010-2011 season, Manchester United were unbeaten at home. They had only one draw, and dropped only 2 points out of a possible 57. Similarly, for other seasons the annotations provide some information regarding the change in managers and how that concided with a drop or improvement in home performance. In 2013-2014 for example, David Moyes was appointed as the new manager of Manchester United, and this resulted in a huge downturn in home performance.
